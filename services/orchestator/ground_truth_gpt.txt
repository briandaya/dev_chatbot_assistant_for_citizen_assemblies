El sistema agroalimentario tiene un triple y contradictorio rol en la crisis ecológica: es una de las causas
The context mentions that the current system predominantly relies on stock resources, such as fossil fuels that can be used at will. However, there is a transition towards a system where flow resources, such as photovoltaic electricity and wind power, become more dominant. Flow resources are characterized by their intermittent nature. This transition will require social management of electric usage and the participation of citizens in the debate on the production system. It will also necessitate the implementation of massive energy storage systems, such as batteries, reversible hydroelectric power plants, and hydrogen. Therefore, the differences between resources of stock and resources of flow lie in their characteristics and the need for social management and storage systems.
